## 📖 Biography

Muhammad was the founding prophet of Islam, born in Mecca around 570 CE. Raised as an orphan, he worked as a merchant and married Khadijah, a wealthy businesswoman. At the age of 40, during meditations in the Hira cave, he received the first divine revelation through the Archangel Gabriel. These revelations continued for 23 years, forming the Quran, the holy book of Islam.

Facing opposition in Mecca, Muhammad emigrated to Medina in 622 CE (Hijra), an event that marks the beginning of the Islamic calendar. There he founded the first Muslim community, unifying Arab tribes under a new religious and political identity. He led armies in defense of the community and expanded Islam's influence throughout the Arabian Peninsula.

Muhammad died in 632 CE, leaving behind not only a world religion but also a model of state, law, and society that shaped Islamic civilization for centuries. His exemplary life (Sunnah) serves as a guide for Muslims in all aspects of life, from spirituality to governance.

## 🏆 Achievements

- **Founder of Islam**: Established the principles and practices of one of the world's largest religions
- **Unifier of Arabia**: United the fragmented tribes of the Arabian Peninsula into a single nation and community
- **Lawgiver**: Developed a complete legal and social system (Sharia) that governs Muslim life
- **Spiritual and Political Leader**: Created the model of theocratic leadership that influenced numerous civilizations
- **Transmitter of the Quran**: Preserved and transmitted divine revelations that form Islam's holy book

## 🌍 Impact and Legacy

Muhammad had the most profound impact on human history, founding a religion with over 1.8 billion followers today. Islam became not just a spiritual force but a civilization that contributed massively to science, philosophy, art, and mathematics. His model of state inspired numerous empires and nations, and Islamic law continues to influence modern legal systems.

His legacy includes the cultural and scientific expansion of the Islamic world, urban development, international trade, and preservation of ancient knowledge. His life and teachings remain a model of moral and social conduct for a third of the world's population.
